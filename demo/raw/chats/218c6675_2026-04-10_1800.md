8	# LoRA vs Full Fine-tuning
9	
10	**Q:** When should I use LoRA vs full fine-tuning?
11	
12	## LoRA
13	- Trains ~0.1% of parameters (low-rank matrices)
14	- Much faster, less GPU memory
15	- Good for adapting to similar domains
16	- Can merge multiple LoRA adapters
17	
18	## Full Fine-tuning
19	- Trains all parameters
20	- Better performance ceiling
21	- More data needed
22	- Risk of catastrophic forgetting
23	
24	**Rule of thumb**: Start with LoRA. Only use full fine-tuning if LoRA performance plateaus and you have enough data.

Your wiki has two pages on finetuning:

**[[Fine-tuning]]** — concept page covering the main methods:
- **Full fine-tuning** — update all params
- **LoRA** — low-rank adaptation, train small matrices
- **Prompt tuning** — learn soft prompt vectors
- **RLHF** — RL from human feedback for alignment

Key point: start from a pre-trained model ([[BERT]], [[GPT]]), fine-tune on your data with much less data than training from scratch.

**[[LoRA vs Full Fine-tuning]]** — answer page with a practical comparison:
- LoRA: ~0.1% of params, faster, less VRAM, good for similar domains, stackable adapters
- Full: higher performance ceiling, more data needed, risk of catastrophic forgetting
- **Rule of thumb**: start with LoRA, only go full if LoRA plateaus and you have enough data
